Travel app

Goeat

Goeat was my final project for UX/UI Bootcamp at Ironhack. This project was selected as finalist among all the students' projects and was presented during Madrid's Hackshow. The project duration was 10 days, 5 days for UX and the other 5 for UI.

The Problem

There's a cultural and linguistic gap that makes harder discovering the gastronomy of a country

Tourism is one of the world biggest economic drivers. During 2018 it grow 6%, reaching 1400 million travelers worldwide, according to the UNWTO – United Nations World Tourism Organization. Within this sector, gastronomic tourism is a growing market. Gastronomy is one of the main reasons tourists chose their destinations, and it represents 1/3 of the total travel costs.

However, there is a linguistic and cultural barrier when discovering local cuisine, making it harder for tourist to enjoy the authenticity of the places they visit through food.

With this project we'll identify the barriers tourist experience, with the goal of improving the experience.

The solution

The solution is Goeat. It's an application that will help you easily discover your destination's tradicional dishes, and suggest nearby restaurant to try them out.

Define

User Persona

I created a user persona, Irene, synthesizing all the information gathered in the surveys and interviews.

"I want to try authentic local things" - Irene

Irene is 31 years old, she likes to travel and discover new cultures. She love to try authentic local things, because it helps her understand the culture both intellectually and from the senses. She doesn't like to find out she missed something once she's back home.

User Journey

I used a User Journey to emphasize with Irene during a trip.

Thanks to this tool we can detect Irene's pain points:

  1. She looses time looking for tradicional food in the destination, and it's hard to choose which restaurant to go to.
  2. Once she arrives at the restaurant, the menu is not in her language and she can't understand it.
  3. She doesn't know the food ingredients nor the dishes, so she doesn't know what to order and ends up ordering without knowing what she'll get.
  4. She gets food with an ingredient she doesn't like and gets frustrated because she couldn't enjoy the experience.

Ideate

Brainstorming

Once the design opportunities were detected, I started thinking about the main app features.

HMW: How might we help Irene enjoy her destination's gastronomy during her trip?

After two Brainstorming rounds, I organized the ideas in a MOSCOW to decide what features would be in my MVP.

  • Geo-localization to suggest tradicional dishes from the city the user's in.
  • Suggest nearby restaurants to try the selected dish.
  • Street food recommendations from other travelers.
  • Dishes names audio pronunciation.
  • Interesting information and curiosities about traditional dishes.
  • Menu translations.
  • Ingredients and allergen information.

Prototype and Iterate

Low Fidelity

Keeping in mind the architecture of the information and the tasks shown in the user flow, I started working on the first Low Fidelity paper version of the app.

I performed a usability test with 5 people. The main pain points were:

  • 80% of the users found weird having to search dishes from a city when the geo-localization information was already giving some data.
  • 60% of the users would like to see restaurant's dishes right in the map view.
  • 60% of the users found hard to choose a restaurant to get the direction instructions. The "go to place" button wasn't clear.

Mid Fidelity

With the learnings from the Low Fidelity, I started iterating in the Mid Fidelity using Sketch.

Once the Mid Fidelity was finished on Sketch, I used inVision to do a second testing round with other 5 people.

  • Missing a screen where the user accepts geo-localization.
  • Some typography was too small.
  • Missing allergen information on the restaurant dish view.
  • 100% of the users wanted a shortcut to their points card from the tab bar.

High Fidelity

I developed the High Fidelity applying the changes discovered during the usability test.

Branding

Moodboard

I developed the app's branding using different resources. The first one was a moodboard, where I added pictures that represented my brand attributes: authentic, inspiring and adventurer.

I did a Desirability Test with 5 users and could confirm that the moodboard represented the brand's attributes.

Style guide

This document gathers all the guidelines and visual references for the design to be consistent and align with the brand's vision.

Typography: Lato is a Sans Serif created by Lukasz Dziedzic with great legibility. The semi-rounded details in the characters provide Lato a warm, formal but friendly feeling.

Colors: Goeat's main colors are red –Amaranth, deep blue -Firefly and grey -Seashell. Red is the primary color.
